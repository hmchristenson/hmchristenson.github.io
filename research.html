About | Research Interests | CV

My research interests include reionization, high redshift galaxies, and the intergalactic medium. I am currently working with George Becker at UCR on understanding the evolution of the intergalactic medium at the epoch of reionization, particularly the source of large-scale fluctuations of its opacity to Lyman alpha photons.

My past research appointments have included REUs at Rutgers University (2015 with Prof. Eric Gawiser) and the Maria Mitchell Observatory (2016 with Dr. Regina Jorgenson) and undergraduate research with Prof. Kristen Larson at Western Washington University. I have experience with integral field spectroscopy, photometric surveys, and quantifying nonlinear sources of uncertainty.
