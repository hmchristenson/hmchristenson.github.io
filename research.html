About | Research Interests | CV

My research interests include interstellar dust, Lyman Alpha emitters, and damped Lyman Alpha systems. Generally, I'm interested in observational astronomy, including uncertainty analysis, the handling and analysis of large datasets, and improving my coding skills!

I am currently at an REU internship at the Maria Mitchell Observatory working with Dr. Regina Jorgenson to search for damped Lyman Alpha systems using integral field spectroscopic data from the OSIRIS spectrograph at Keck. I plan to present our results at the 2017 winter AAS meeting.

In the summer of 2015, I participated in an REU at Rutgers University and continue to work with Prof. Eric Gawiser remotely. We are working on cataloguing Lyman Alpha emitting galaxies at redshift z=3.1 in the MUSYC1030+05 field. I presented our preliminary results at the winter 2016 AAS meeting.

At WWU, I work with Prof. Kristen Larson on quantifying uncertainty in the extinction-to-reddening ratio in the near-infrared due to uncertainty in the spectral type of main-sequence background stars. I presented our findings at the winter 2015 AAS meeting and a paper on our work is currently under review.
